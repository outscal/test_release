## Narrative Structure: 5-Beat Arc (~200 Words)

### Beat 1: HOOK (1 line, 15 words max)
Bold technical claim, comparison, or "secret" mechanism reveal.

**Hook Patterns (replace [Subject] with your actual topic from research):**
- "This is how [Subject] secretly [mechanism]"
- "Why does [Subject A]'s [approach] make [Subject B] look broken?"
- "[Subject] literally [extreme technical claim]"
- "You won't believe how [Subject] [technical detail]"
- "This is the [superlative] way [Subject] has ever [achievement]"
- "[Subject] was so [adjective] that it nearly [extreme consequence]"
- "[Subject] has been lying about being [perceived quality]"
- "Did you know [Subject] is [designed/built] to [counterintuitive action]?"

**Examples from dataset (gaming - shows STYLE, not required topic):**
- "This game literally scars you for every mistake you make."
- "Why does Ghost of Tsushima's fake cloth physics make Batman's cape look broken?"
- "Batman's cape was so complex that it nearly burned down the PlayStation 3."

**How these patterns work for OTHER topics:**
- AI: "This is how ChatGPT secretly forgets everything you tell it."
- Finance: "Why does index investing make hedge funds look broken?"
- Science: "CRISPR was so powerful that it nearly got banned worldwide."
- Culture: "TikTok's algorithm literally rewires how Gen Z learns language."

### Beat 2: CONTEXT / PROBLEM (1-2 lines)
Explain what "most [things in this category]" do (the lazy/broken way).

**Pattern:** "See, most [category] [lazy solution]. But [Smart Subject]..."

**Examples from dataset (gaming):**
- "See, most AAA games brute force cloth simulation with expensive real-time physics, but still end up with capes that spaz out and destroy your CPU."
- "You see, most racing games just make the track wet and call it realistic weather."

**How this pattern works for OTHER topics:**
- Tech: "See, most APIs just return error codes and call it error handling. But this system..."
- Finance: "You see, most investors just buy the dip and hope. But this strategy..."
- Science: "See, most studies just measure correlation. But this research..."
- Culture: "See, most slang takes decades to spread. But TikTok's algorithm..."

### Beat 3: TECHNICAL DEEP DIVE (Main section) - THE MEAT
Explain the clever solution with specific terminology - this is where the "wow factor" lives.

**ðŸš¨ THIS IS THE MOST IMPORTANT SECTION. This is what separates good shorts from forgettable ones.**

**Requirements:**
- Use real technical terms from YOUR topic (not gaming terms for non-gaming topics)
- **Explain the MECHANISM, not just the outcome** (HOW it works, not just WHAT it does)
- Keep it visual (describe things that could be shown)
- One concrete example or proof point

**Technical terms vary by topic - use terms from YOUR research:**
- Gaming: voxels, vertices, GPU instancing, shaders
- AI: transformers, attention mechanisms, token limits, fine-tuning
- Finance: yield curves, liquidity pools, arbitrage, derivatives
- Biology: CRISPR, gene expression, protein folding
- Culture: algorithm, engagement metrics, virality coefficient

**ðŸš¨ THE MECHANISM RULE:**

Every technical claim MUST explain the underlying mechanism. Don't just state facts - explain WHY.

**âŒ BAD (Surface-level):**
> "BeamNG calculates 2000 times per second."

**âœ… GOOD (Explains the mechanism):**
> "Each of BeamNG's four thousand beams has four properties: stiffness, damping, deform threshold, and snap point. The engine recalculates all of them two thousand times per second. That's why the same crash never looks the same twice."

**âŒ BAD (Just numbers):**
> "The game uses 400 nodes and 4000 beams."

**âœ… GOOD (Numbers with meaning):**

**The Mechanism Formula:**
```
[Technical fact] + [How it actually works] + [Why that matters]
```

**Examples:**
- "Temporal rewind. When you shoot, the server rewinds to what was on YOUR screen, not where enemies are NOW. That's why blood appears behind enemies you just killed."
