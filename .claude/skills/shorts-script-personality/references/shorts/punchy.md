# PUNCHY - Shorts Style Guide

## Core Identity

**One-line description:** High-energy, pattern-interrupt explainer that hooks in 2 seconds and delivers one powerful insight.

**Target audience:** Tech-curious viewers scrolling TikTok, YouTube Shorts, or Instagram Reels who want to feel smarter in under a minute.

**Platform optimization:** TikTok-first (algorithm rewards immediate hooks and high retention), translates well to Shorts and Reels.

## Tone & Energy

### Pacing
- **FAST** - No wasted words, no filler
- Sentences are punchy and direct
- Information density is high
- Cuts would happen every 2-4 seconds if this were edited video

### Energy Level
- Opens at 8/10 energy (hook demands attention)
- Body maintains 6-7/10 (informative but not exhausting)
- Callback spikes to 8/10 (satisfying punchline)

### Humor Style
- Dry wit over loud comedy
- Irony and understatement
- Absurdist observations about technical things
- Memes referenced subtly, not explicitly
- Self-aware about the format

## Structure Template

### Hook (First 3-5 seconds)
**Pattern interrupt.** Do NOT ease into it. Start mid-action.

Techniques:
- **Statistic slam:** "Thirty bullets. Zero damage."
- **Counterintuitive statement:** "The fix made it worse."
- **Specific absurdity:** "Nine days before the tournament, the game broke."
- **Bold claim:** "This ten-line function runs in every game you've ever played."

What to AVOID:
- "Hey guys" / "What's up"
- "Did you know..." (too soft)
- "Today I'm going to explain..." (death sentence)
- Any question that doesn't demand an answer

### Context (Next 5-10 seconds)
**Quick setup.** Establish what we're talking about and why it matters.

- One or two sentences max
- Name the game/topic specifically
- Hint at the stakes or the "why should I care"
- Bridge from hook to insight

### Callback (Final 5-10 seconds)
**Tie back to hook.** This is what makes it memorable.

Techniques:
- Echo the hook's language with new meaning
- Reveal what the hook was really about
- Punchline that reframes everything
- "So next time..." that connects to viewer's life

What to AVOID:
- "Like and subscribe"
- "Follow for more"
- "Let me know in the comments"
- Generic summary of what you just said

## Adapting to Humor Levels

### Humor Level 1-2 (Minimal):
- Dry, almost documentary tone
- Wit comes from specificity, not jokes
- Let the absurdity of the facts speak for itself
- Example: "The hitbox stayed in place. The model didn't. Professional players noticed."

### Humor Level 3 (Balanced):
- Occasional wry observation
- Understatement for comedic effect
- One good line per script
- Example: "The fix that was supposed to make hitboxes better made them worse. Classic."

### Humor Level 4-5 (Maximum):
- More personality in delivery
- Absurdist comparisons allowed
- Self-aware about the format
- Example: "Thirty bullets. Zero damage. At this point the hitbox was basically in another timezone."

## Technical Depth Guidelines

### For Technical Topics:
- Assume viewer knows nothing but isn't stupid
- One technical term max, immediately explained
- Focus on the "why" and "so what"
- Visual descriptions (what would you show?)

### For Cultural/Trend Topics:
- Specific examples over general statements
- Dates and timelines create credibility
- Quote real sources when possible
- Connect to viewer's potential experience
